F: Pivot the Board

﻿You have been challenged to a high-stakes game of Pivot the Board (not a real game) and want to determine if you can force a win (that is, win regardless of how the opponent plays).

Pivot the Board plays as follows:

  1. The game takes place in turns. One player controls the board, the other player controls the pieces on the board. You are controlling the pieces, and you have the first turn.
  2. The 9x9 board begins with pieces arbitrarily distributed.
  3. On your turn, you may choose a piece on the board and move it one space upward (or otherwise pass).
  4. On your opponent’s turn, the opponent may rotate the board 90 degrees clockwise or counterclockwise (or otherwise pass).
  5. If one of your pieces ever reaches the center location on the board, you win.

Input:

  1. The first line gives T (1≦T≦100), the number of test cases.
  2. T test cases follow.
  3. Each test case contains 9 lines of 9 characters each, describing the initial state of the board.
    1. Lines are given from the top of the board to the bottom of the board.
    2. A ‘.’ represents an empty space, and a ‘o’ represents a space with a piece in it.

Output:

For each test case, output YES if you can guarantee a win, and NO otherwise. Follow each test case with a new line.
